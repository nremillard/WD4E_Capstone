Non-Stop Eating

Giant pandas spend 10 to 16 hours a day eating, mainly bamboo. They eat for so long because they don't store fat and don't hibernate.

Giant Panda (Photo: Pixabay)

Solitary Lifestyle

Pandas are solitary creatures and enjoy their own company. They have a sense of smell that alerts them to other pandas so they can avoid them.

Resting Panda (Photo: Pixabay)

Extended Wrist Bone

Giant pandas have an extended wrist bone on their front paws that they use like a thumb to grip food and hold bamboo stems.

Sleeping Panda (Photo: Pixabay)

Verticle Pupils

Giant pandas have vertical slits for pupils, similar to domestic cats.

Giant Panda Covered in Bamboo (Photo: Pixabay)
